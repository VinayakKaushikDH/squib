pet.pick

18 candidates · pick one species
goal. settle the species + art vocabulary before building variants. a coding helper pet needs to: read well at 64–120 px, emote across 6+ states, survive on any wallpaper, and not annoy you after 8 hours of staring at it. below are 18 candidates organized by art style first, not species. once you pick a row you like, next pass explodes it into 4-5 finished variants.
A what makes a good coding-helper pet

Read at a glance

Silhouette must carry meaning at 64px. Round, blocky, or strongly asymmetric silhouettes win; skinny stick-figures vanish.

Expressive face

Two dots for eyes + one mouth shape can do 80% of the work. Big head-to-body ratio reads cuter and emotes clearer.

Fits the work

Nocturnal / patient / tinkering creatures feel right for debugging at 2am. Not hyper-active. Loyal-companion energy.

05 My take

If I'm picking — I'd pare down to these four and do another round on one:

  • 04 Squib octopus (pixel) — the name is right there, 8 arms animate gorgeously, matches the reference vocabulary. Safest creative win.
  • 07 Axo axolotl (vector) — softest, most "cute" without being juvenile. Built-in animation points (gills).
  • 17 Caret cursor-creature — most ownable, most on-theme for a coding app. Could become a real identity.
  • 05 Dusty moth (pixel) — wildcard. Nocturnal, drawn to screen glow, "debugger" joke lands. Very squib-energy.

Crab stays on the table but it's treading on the reference's species directly. If we go crab, we should commit to a radically different art style (e.g. 13 line/zine crab vs 01 pixel crab) rather than just re-colored pixels.
